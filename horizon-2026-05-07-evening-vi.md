Kiran Kumbhar's essay on Aeon explores how recent genetic studies support long-held historical views that ancient India was shaped by successive waves of migration and population mixing across millennia. This convergence of genetic evidence with historical scholarship provides a more complete picture of South Asian population history, challenging earlier narratives of cultural isolation and static societies. The essay highlights how ancient DNA sequencing, when cross-referenced with modern genetic populations, reveals migration patterns that align with historical records of trade routes and cultural exchanges spanning thousands of years.

rss · Aeon · May 7, 10:00

**Background**: Archaeogenetics is an interdisciplinary field that combines molecular genetic methods with archaeological evidence to study ancient population migrations and evolutionary history. The field, named by archaeologist Colin Renfrew, extracts DNA from fossilized specimens including bones and preserved tissues. By comparing ancient DNA with modern genetic relatives, researchers can reconstruct population movements even when original genetic material is degraded or incomplete.
