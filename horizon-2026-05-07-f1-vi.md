## [F1 drivers say they are still at the mercy of their power units over a flying lap | Formula 1](https://www.racefans.net/2026/05/07/f1-drivers-say-they-are-still-at-the-mercy-of-their-power-units-over-a-flying-lap/) ⭐️ 3.0/10

F1 drivers report that the 2026 technical regulations have not resolved issues with power unit behavior affecting performance during qualifying laps, and one peculiar characteristic introduced by these rules remains unfixed after last weekend's changes. This matters because driver qualifying performance is now heavily dependent on power unit characteristics rather than pure driver skill, potentially creating competitive imbalances between teams with different power unit suppliers. The 2026 regulations introduced refinements including a boost power cap at +150 kW in race conditions (or the car's current power level if higher), and allows drivers to recharge an extra +0.5MJ for sustained higher speeds, yet drivers still feel at the mercy of their power units during qualifying laps.

**Background**: Since 2014, F1 has used hybrid power units combining a V6 turbo engine with energy recovery systems (ERS) that capture energy from braking and exhaust heat. These complex systems can operate in different modes with varying power outputs. Qualifying modes, nicknamed 'party modes,' make available extra engine revs and allow maximum deployment of recovered energy without harvesting to the battery. A separate cost cap for power unit manufacturers was increased from $95 million to $130 million from 2026 onwards to support development of the new hybrid engines.

## [Kimi Antonelli has "earned the right" to lead F1 title race, says David Coulthard](https://www.motorsport.com/f1/news/kimi-antonelli-has-earned-the-right-to-lead-f1-title-race-says-david-coulthard/10818745/?utm_source=RSS&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=RSS-F1&utm_term=News&utm_content=www) ⭐️ 3.0/10

Former F1 driver David Coulthard has endorsed Mercedes driver Kimi Antonelli as having 'earned the right' to lead the drivers' championship. The 19-year-old became the youngest driver in F1 history to lead the standings after winning the Chinese and Japanese Grands Prix consecutively. Antonelli's rapid rise at just 19 years old represents a potential generational shift in F1's competitive landscape. His championship lead positions him as a central figure in what could become a defining season for young talent in the sport. Antonelli extended his championship lead after winning the Miami Grand Prix, further solidifying his position. Coulthard's endorsement carries particular weight given his own experience as a former F1 race winner and team principal.

**Background**: Formula 1 is the highest level of single-seater motorsport, featuring the fastest racing cars in the world. Kimi Antonelli drives for Mercedes, one of F1's most prestigious teams, and has rapidly progressed through the racing ranks. David Coulthard raced in F1 from 1994 to 2008, winning 13 Grand Prix races and later serving as a team principal at Red Bull Racing.
