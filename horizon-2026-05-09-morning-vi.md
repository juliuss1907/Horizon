## [Google broke reCAPTCHA for de-googled Android users](https://reclaimthenet.org/google-broke-recaptcha-for-de-googled-android-users) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Google has broken reCAPTCHA for users running de-googled Android distributions by implementing remote attestation, which verifies device authenticity through Google's infrastructure. This change forces privacy-conscious users to either abandon their preferred privacy setup, use workarounds, or lose access to services protected by reCAPTCHA. This development represents a significant escalation in Google's push toward hardware-backed device verification, effectively locking out users who have chosen to opt out of Google's ecosystem. It sets a concerning precedent where legitimate privacy choices may become technically infeasible as more services adopt attestation-based verification. The attestation chain works through three stages: EK (Endorsement Key - a static burned-in private key unique to each device) → AIK (Attestation Identity Key - an ephemeral identity key created and signed by a Google server) → attestation (cryptographic proof signed by AIK). With Google servers logging EK-to-AIK conversions, they can track and identify individual devices regardless of software modifications.

hackernews · anonymousiam · May 8, 18:45

**Background**: reCAPTCHA is Google's challenge-response system used to distinguish human users from bots on websites. De-googled Android refers to custom Android distributions like GrapheneOS that remove Google Play Services and other proprietary Google components for privacy. Remote attestation is a Trusted Computing mechanism that allows external parties to verify the integrity and configuration of a device's hardware and software. This technology has drawn criticism from privacy advocates who view it as a way for hardware manufacturers and software providers to enforce control over users' computing environments.

## [Engineers at NASA's Jet Propulsion Lab make a breakthrough in rotor technology](https://arstechnica.com/space/2026/05/engineers-at-nasas-jet-propulsion-lab-make-a-breakthrough-in-rotor-technology/) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Engineers at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory have achieved a breakthrough enabling rotor blades to withstand supersonic speeds without disintegrating during testing. The SkyFall rotor system, featuring a two-bladed design, reached near-supersonic speeds at 3,570 rpm during testing. This breakthrough could enable future Mars rotorcraft to exceed the Mach 1 barrier that was carefully avoided during Ingenuity's missions. The capability to design rotors that safely operate at supersonic tip speeds opens new possibilities for larger, faster planetary aircraft capable of covering greater distances. The new rotor design builds upon the carbon-fiber technology used in Ingenuity, which spun its blades at 2,700 rpm—already 10 times faster than passenger helicopters on Earth. Engineers had previously avoided exceeding Mach 1 on Mars (approximately 540 mph) out of concern that blade overspeed might cause catastrophic shattering.

rss · Ars Technica · May 8, 15:46

**Background**: Rotor blade disintegration at high speeds occurs due to extreme centrifugal forces and aerodynamic stresses that exceed the structural limits of the blade material. When rotors spin near the speed of sound, shock waves can form along the blade surfaces, creating unstable conditions that cause fatigue and eventual failure. Ingenuity's design team deliberately limited its rotor speed to avoid exceeding Mach 1, a constraint that the new JPL breakthrough has now overcome.
