## [GPT-5.5 Matches Mythos Preview in Cybersecurity Benchmarks](https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/05/amid-mythos-hyped-cybersecurity-prowess-researchers-find-gpt-5-5-is-just-as-good/) ⭐️ 6.0/10

New research reveals that OpenAI's GPT-5.5 performs comparably to Anthropic's heavily hyped Claude Mythos Preview in cybersecurity benchmarks. The findings suggest that the advanced cyber threat capabilities demonstrated by Mythos are not unique to any single AI model, challenging the notion of a breakthrough specific to one provider. This comparison is significant because it undermines claims that Mythos possesses uniquely powerful cybersecurity capabilities. For security researchers and policymakers, the results suggest that advanced AI-enabled cyber threats are becoming more accessible across multiple models, raising concerns about the broader democratization of sophisticated attack capabilities. Claude Mythos Preview previously scored 83.1% on the CyberGym benchmark, one of the industry's most rigorous cybersecurity assessment tools. According to the new findings, GPT-5.5 achieved comparable scores on the same benchmark, suggesting that similar cybersecurity competencies can be achieved across different model architectures. Notably, Mythos has only been made available to a restricted consortium of 40+ technology companies due to Anthropic's concerns about potential misuse.

**Background**: Claude Mythos Preview was unveiled by Anthropic in early April 2026 as a frontier model specifically designed for cybersecurity applications. The company claimed it was so effective at revealing software vulnerabilities that they feared releasing it publicly, as it could fall into the hands of malicious actors. Instead, Anthropic made it available only to a select group of partners including Apple, Amazon, and other major technology firms through a private consortium. The model also achieved impressive scores on other benchmarks, including 93.9% on SWE-bench Verified and 97.6% on the USAMO math olympiad.
