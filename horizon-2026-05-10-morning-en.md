## [Zed Editor Launches Theme Builder for Easier Customization](https://zed.dev/theme-builder) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Zed Editor released a Theme Builder tool that allows users to easily create and customize editor themes through a visual interface. Users can adjust syntax colors, UI elements, and other theme components without writing code, with community members reporting they created their own themes in just a few minutes. This tool lowers the barrier for theme customization, potentially attracting developers who found Zed's default themes insufficient. It addresses longstanding requests for better theming control and could help Zed compete more directly with VSCode's extensive theme ecosystem, especially as users report that default dark themes feel low contrast and dull. The tool was developed by Zed Industries and released at zed.dev/theme-builder. Community feedback indicates syntax highlighting for C/C++ remains limited, UI text configuration has only two settings available, and smooth scrolling is not yet supported—all areas where users hope for improvements.

hackernews · cuechan · May 9, 17:30

**Background**: Zed is an open-source code editor for Linux, macOS, and Windows written in Rust by Nathan Sobo, one of the creators of Atom. It leverages Tree-sitter for syntax parsing and highlighting, and supports real-time collaborative editing. The editor has been gaining traction among developers, with some users reporting it has become their preferred choice after 15 years of using vim.

## [Japan May Be Selling US Treasuries to Defend the Yen](https://t.me/quantin_vic/201794) ⭐️ 6.0/10

The Federal Reserve's holdings of US Treasuries dropped by $8.7 billion to $2.73 trillion in the week ending May 6. Bloomberg analysis indicates this aligns with Japan selling US government debt to finance purchasing an estimated $54.7 billion of yen during the same period. Japan, as the largest foreign holder of US government debt, continuing to sell Treasuries could add further pressure to US bond yields already rising due to oil price spikes and fiscal deficit concerns. This intervention dynamic could affect global debt markets and potentially increase US government borrowing costs. Bank of America notes that Japan did not tap cash reserves in previous interventions, implying financing comes entirely from bond sales. If this pattern repeats, the total impact on Treasury supply from the current intervention cycle could reach approximately $70 billion.

**Background**: Currency intervention occurs when a central bank or government directly buys or sells foreign currency to influence exchange rate movements. Japan has a long history of intervening in currency markets to prevent excessive yen appreciation or depreciation, having spent over $200 billion defending the yen since 2022. Bond yields represent the annual return an investor receives relative to a bond's current market price, and when bond prices fall (supply increases), yields rise correspondingly.
