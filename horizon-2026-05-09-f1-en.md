## [Gary Anderson Questions 2027 F1 Engine Regulation Changes](https://www.the-race.com/formula-1/gary-anderson-f1s-engine-numbers-still-dont-add-up/) ⭐️ 3.0/10

F1 technical analyst Gary Anderson has publicly questioned whether the planned 2027 engine regulation changes will effectively solve the sport's underlying problems, despite FIA and F1 bosses agreeing to modifications that shift the power split between internal combustion and electrical systems. The 2027 engine regulations will shape Formula 1's competitive balance and sustainability for years to come. If Anderson's skepticism is validated, teams and manufacturers may face continued challenges in creating entertaining, high-performance racing while meeting environmental and cost targets. The agreed changes involve increasing ICE power by approximately 50kW with a corresponding fuel-flow increase, while reducing Energy Recovery System (ERS) deployment power by approximately 50kW. Anderson, known for his deep technical expertise as a former Jordan and BAR chief engineer, believes the numerical adjustments don't address what he sees as fundamental issues in the power unit formula.

rss · The Race F1 · May 9, 07:12

**Background**: Since 2014, F1 has operated with hybrid turbocharged power units combining internal combustion engines with energy recovery systems. The 2026 regulations marked the most substantial transformation since that era began. The current debate centers on finding the right balance between ICE and electrical power to create 'more intuitive' driving experiences while maintaining the spectacle and technological relevance that attracts manufacturers and fans.
