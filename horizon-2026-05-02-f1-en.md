## [Ferrari Halo Wing Returns for 2025, Banned from 2027](https://www.the-race.com/formula-1/ferrari-halo-wing-miami-gp-f1-legal-2027-ban/) ⭐️ 4.0/10

Ferrari has brought back its halo wing aerodynamic device for the 2025 season at the Miami Grand Prix, but this device is set to be banned by 2027 under new Formula 1 regulations being introduced by the FIA. This regulatory timeline highlights the ongoing tension between technical innovation and safety standardization in F1, as teams continuously find ways to exploit safety devices for aerodynamic advantages while the FIA works to prevent the halo from becoming a performance optimization tool. The halo was made mandatory in F1 starting from the 2018 season as a safety measure to protect drivers' heads during crashes, and since its introduction, teams have progressively developed aerodynamic appendages attached to it to gain competitive advantages.

**Background**: The halo is a titanium safety device mounted around the cockpit of single-seater racing cars, consisting of curved metal bars forming a protective arch over the driver's head. It was introduced to F1 in 2018 after incidents highlighted the vulnerability of drivers' heads to impact. The halo has since been adopted by other open-wheel racing series including Formula E, Formula 2, Formula 3, and IndyCar. Since the halo's introduction, teams have been finding ways to add aerodynamic elements to it, blurring the line between safety device and performance tool.
