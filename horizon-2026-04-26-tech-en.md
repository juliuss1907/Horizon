iPhone users report that the Headspace meditation app silently reinstalls itself daily around 1pm EST, even when automatic downloads are disabled in iOS settings. If an app can reinstall itself despite disabled automatic downloads, it undermines fundamental user control over device management—a concern that could theoretically affect any iOS user with a purchase history on the App Store. Reports began approximately 2-3 days before the HN post, affecting users who previously purchased Headspace. The reinstallations appear to bypass the Automatic Downloads toggle in Settings > App Store. Historically, Headspace was involved in a notable 2017 iOS bug where local time-based notifications caused endless crash loops, with meditation apps being particularly affected due to their daily reminder notifications.

**Background**: iOS includes an automatic downloads feature that can reinstall previously purchased apps on any device logged into the same Apple ID. Users can control this through Settings > App Store > Automatic Downloads. This feature is separate from Background App Refresh, which only allows suspended apps to check for content updates without reinstalling. The feature is designed for convenience but has been reported to malfunction or behave unexpectedly under certain conditions, potentially causing unwanted reinstallations.
