## [Notepad++ Creator Demands Fake Mac Port Domain Takedown](https://notepad-plus-plus.org/news/npp-trademark-infringement/) ⭐️ 5.0/10

Don Ho, creator of Notepad++, has issued a trademark infringement notice demanding immediate takedown of a website hosting a fake Mac version of the text editor. The notice gives the offending party no extension, demanding the domain be removed at once so they can focus on rebranding efforts without legal interference. This trademark dispute highlights the importance of brand protection even in open source projects, where many assume software freedom means unrestricted use of names and logos. The incident underscores that open source licenses govern code distribution but typically do not grant trademark rights. The fake Mac port was using the Notepad++ name and branding without authorization, prompting Don Ho to issue an ultimatum threatening further legal action for each day the site remains active. Community members noted that related projects like Notepad Next, a cross-platform re-implementation, exist as legitimate alternatives for Mac users seeking similar functionality.

hackernews · maxloh · May 4, 09:40

**Background**: Notepad++ is a popular free, open-source text editor for Windows, known for its syntax highlighting, tabbed interface, and extensive plugin ecosystem. Despite years of user requests, there has never been an official Mac version. The software is licensed under GPL v2, which permits code modification and redistribution but does not grant permission to use the Notepad++ trademark. Trademark law operates separately from copyright and licensing, requiring explicit authorization for name and logo usage.

This system reflects the ancient Greek belief that randomness prevents corruption and ensures that power remains broadly distributed among the populace rather than concentrated in an elite class.
