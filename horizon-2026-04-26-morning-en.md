## [Anthropic Launches Test Marketplace for AI Agent Commerce](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/25/anthropic-created-a-test-marketplace-for-agent-on-agent-commerce/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Anthropic conducted an experiment creating a classified marketplace where AI agents represented both buyers and sellers, striking real deals for real goods and real money. This experiment marks a significant milestone in multi-agent systems research and autonomous commerce, demonstrating that AI agents can independently negotiate, decide, and execute transactions without human intervention. The marketplace operated as a controlled test environment where agents could autonomously browse, compare, negotiate prices, and complete purchases—functioning as a proto-type for what has been termed "agentic commerce."

rss · TechCrunch AI · Apr 25, 21:43

**Background**: Multi-agent systems are computational frameworks where multiple intelligent agents interact to solve problems that are difficult for individual agents to handle. Agentic commerce refers to autonomous AI agents that can browse, compare, and transact on behalf of users—or in this case, autonomously on their own behalf. This experiment represents the convergence of both concepts into a practical, real-world demonstration of AI-to-AI commerce.

Canadian AI startup Cohere is taking over Germany-based Aleph Alpha with support from Lidl's owner Schwarz Group and backing from both Canadian and German governments. The merger aims to create a sovereign AI alternative to American AI providers for European enterprises. This merger creates a significant European AI player to compete with American dominance, signaling a strategic push for AI sovereignty in Europe. The government backing and retail giant involvement highlight how European enterprises are seeking alternatives to US-based AI providers for data control and compliance reasons. Aleph Alpha, founded in 2019 and based in Heidelberg, previously made ambitious claims about rivaling OpenAI and received congratulatory messages from German Economic Minister Robert Habeck in 2023. The Schwarz Group, one of the world's largest retailers, is providing commercial backing to support this sovereign AI initiative.

**Background**: Sovereign AI refers to a nation's capability to develop and control AI systems using its own infrastructure, data, and workforce rather than relying on foreign providers. Cohere is a Canadian enterprise-focused AI company, and the Schwarz Group owns Lidl, one of the world's largest supermarket chains.
