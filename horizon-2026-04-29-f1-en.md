## [Audi Plans Major F1 Engine Concept Change to Address Performance Issues](https://www.the-race.com/formula-1/audi-first-f1-engine-biggest-problems/) ⭐️ 4.0/10

## [Aston Martin Retains AMR26 in Japan for Honda Vibration Dyno Testing](https://www.motorsport.com/f1/news/f1-honda-after-suzuka-one-amr26-remained-in-japan-to-eliminate-vibrations-on-the-dynamometer/10816312/?utm_source=RSS&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=RSS-F1&utm_term=News&utm_content=www) ⭐️ 4.0/10

Aston Martin chose to keep one of their AMR26 Formula 1 cars in Japan following the Suzuka Grand Prix to conduct extensive dynamometer testing, specifically targeting vibration issues originating from Honda's power unit. The testing aims to eliminate these vibrations before the car returns to the race track. This testing represents a significant technical intervention during the racing season, as vibration issues can compromise driver confidence, accelerate component wear, and negatively impact aerodynamic performance. Resolving these issues quickly is crucial for Aston Martin's competitiveness in the 2025 championship standings. A dynamometer measures torque and rotational speed of an engine to calculate instantaneous power output, and is particularly useful for identifying noise, vibration, and harshness (NVH) issues. The AMR26 has reportedly logged very little track time before the season, making it critical to address power unit reliability issues early in the development cycle.

rss · Motorsport F1 · Apr 29, 10:14

**Background**: Since 2014, Formula One has used 1.6-liter V6 turbocharged hybrid power units that combine an internal combustion engine with two electric motor-generator units (MGUs) for energy recovery. Honda supplies power units to Aston Martin as part of a technical partnership. Dynamometer testing allows teams to simulate race conditions and identify mechanical issues in a controlled laboratory environment without the variables of actual track running.

## [Hakkinen's Last-Lap Heartbreak at 2001 Spanish GP](https://www.autosport.com/f1/news/on-this-day-hakkinens-last-lap-heartbreak/10816303/?utm_source=RSS&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=RSS-F1&utm_term=News&utm_content=uk) ⭐️ 3.0/10

On April 29, 2001, Mika Hakkinen retired from the lead of the Spanish Grand Prix on the very last lap, during what would be his final Formula 1 season. This marked a poignant end to a difficult season for the two-time world champion, whose retirement symbolized the struggles Hakkinen faced throughout 2001 before his eventual departure from the sport. Before the Spanish GP, Hakkinen had already crashed out of the Australian GP due to a suspension failure while running second, finished a lowly sixth in a mixed-weather Sepang race, and stalled on the grid at Interlagos circuit (also known by its nickname in São Paulo).

**Background**: Mika Hakkinen was a Finnish Formula 1 driver who won back-to-back world championships in 1998 and 1999 with McLaren. The 2001 season proved challenging as he struggled with performance and consistency. The Spanish Grand Prix takes place at the Circuit de Catalunya near Barcelona, and when a driver stalls on the starting grid, it typically means the car's engine dies and the driver must restart from their designated grid position or the pit lane.
