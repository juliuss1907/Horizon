## [Internet Archive Switzerland Launches as Independent European Chapter](https://internetarchive.ch/) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Internet Archive Switzerland has launched as a new independent regional chapter, joining Internet Archive Canada and Internet Archive Europe in building distributed digital preservation infrastructure. The organization is registered as a separate Swiss entity with Brewster and Caslon (founders of the US-based Internet Archive) serving on its board. The launch strengthens the distributed digital library network, providing resilience against political and legal threats that could affect the central US organization. This regional expansion demonstrates a growing federated approach to preserving humanity's digital heritage across borders. Internet Archive Switzerland appears more independent than Internet Archive Canada, which operated like a subsidiary (sharing Slack, email domain, and directors with the US organization). The Swiss chapter's website initially experienced loading difficulties, and community members noted its relevance given ongoing political threats to the Archive in the United States.

**Background**: The Internet Archive is a non-profit digital library founded in 1996, known for its Wayback Machine that archives web pages and its vast collection of digitized books, videos, and other media. Digital preservation involves planning and applying strategies like refreshment, migration, and emulation to ensure long-term access to digital content despite technological change. Distributed digital library infrastructure distributes content and services across multiple locations to enhance resilience and reduce dependence on any single point of failure.
