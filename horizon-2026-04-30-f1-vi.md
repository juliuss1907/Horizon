## [Why a US law could stop the 2026 Miami F1 Grand Prix if weather forecasts are correct](https://www.motorsport.com/f1/news/the-law-that-would-cancel-the-miami-f1-grand-prix-if-the-predictions-come-true/10816519/?utm_source=RSS&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=RSS-F1&utm_term=News&utm_content=www) ⭐️ 3.0/10

AccuWeather forecasts predict an 88% chance of rain and a 53% chance of thunderstorms for the 2026 Miami F1 Grand Prix race day, triggering potential compliance issues with US law requirements for major outdoor public events. This highlights a unique regulatory difference between the US and other countries where F1 operates, as American law mandates specific weather safety thresholds that could force event cancellation regardless of race director discretion. The critical threshold is the 53% thunderstorm probability, which has teams and drivers on alert. Unlike other countries where F1 race officials have flexibility in weather decisions, US regulations require outdoor events to meet mandatory safety protocols during severe weather conditions.

**Background**: Formula 1 races have faced weather challenges before, particularly in Miami where tropical conditions are common. The Miami International Autodrome, located in Miami Gardens, Florida, hosts the event each year and has previously experienced race disruptions due to heavy rainfall. US event safety laws differ significantly from FIA regulations used at European circuits, giving local authorities more power to mandate cancellations based on weather predictions.
