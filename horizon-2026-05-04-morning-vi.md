## [BYOMesh – New LoRa mesh radio offers 100x the bandwidth](https://partyon.xyz/@nullagent/116499715071759135) ⭐️ 6.0/10

BYOMesh is a newly announced LoRa mesh radio that claims to offer 100 times the bandwidth of traditional LoRa solutions, targeting IoT and mesh networking applications. Community members have questioned this claim and raised concerns about FCC regulatory compliance issues affecting similar mesh protocols. This announcement matters because it challenges the traditional trade-off between range and bandwidth in LoRa technology, potentially enabling higher data-rate applications for mesh networks. However, unverified technical claims and unresolved FCC compliance concerns have created significant skepticism in the technical community. BYOMesh operates on the 2.4GHz frequency band, the same as consumer WiFi, which raises questions about traditional LoRa's range advantages at these frequencies. Commenters note that similar mesh protocols like MeshCore and Meshtastic reportedly face FCC compliance issues, and achieving 100x bandwidth through non-compliant methods is not equivalent to achieving it legally.

hackernews · nullagent · May 3, 18:03

**Background**: LoRa (from 'long range') is a physical proprietary radio communication technique based on Chirp Spread Spectrum (CSS) modulation technology. Traditionally, LoRa operates at lower frequencies (e.g., 915MHz in the US) to achieve long-range transmission up to 15km with low power consumption and battery life exceeding 10 years. LoRaWAN is the MAC layer protocol built on top of LoRa modulation, used for low-power wide-area networking (LPWAN) in IoT applications. Higher frequency bands like 2.4GHz offer more bandwidth but typically sacrifice range due to poorer penetration through obstacles.
